Adobo Recipe

A Filipino Staple

Adobo is a popular Filipino meal that is typically made with chicken or pork. White vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, peppercorns, and bay leaves are pantry staples that are used to make a marinade for this dish. The meat is marinated in this combination for an entire night before being simmered on the stovetop in the same marinade. The meat is typically fried in oil before serving after being cooked to perfection in the sauce, which is a distinguishing feature of the cooking technique. The adobo cooking method is believed to have started during the days before refrigerators, when people tried to preserve meat by cooking it in vinegar and salt (or soy sauce in modern renditions). This traditional Filipino dish has countless variations.


Ingredients

  • Pork
  • Onion
  • Dried Bay Leaves
  • Whole Peppercorn
  • Sugar

Steps

  1. Combine the pork belly, soy sauce, and garlic then marinade for at least 1 hour
  2. Heat the pot and put-in the marinated pork belly then cook for a few minutes
  3. Pour remaining marinade including garlic.
  4. Add water, whole pepper corn, and dried bay leaves then bring to a boil. Simmer for 40 minutes to 1 hour
  5. Put-in the vinegar and simmer for 12 to 15 minutes
  6. Add salt to taste
  7. Serve hot. Share and enjoy!
